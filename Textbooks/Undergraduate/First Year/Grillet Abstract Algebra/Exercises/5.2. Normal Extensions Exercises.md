1. 
We have that ${} \Irr(\sqrt[3]{2}:\mathbb{Q})=x^{3}-2 {}$. It's other roots are therefore
$$
\frac{-1-i\sqrt{3}}{\sqrt[3]{4}},\, \frac{-1+i\sqrt{3}}{\sqrt[3]{4}}
$$
which are the conjugates of ${} \sqrt[3]{2} {}$ over $\mathbb{Q}$.
