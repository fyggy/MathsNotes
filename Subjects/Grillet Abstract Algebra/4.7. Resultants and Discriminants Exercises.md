1. 
We have that 
$$
\Res (f, g)=\left| \begin{pmatrix}
 1 & a & b & 0 \\
0 & 1 & a & b \\
1 & p & q & 0 \\
0 & 1 & p & q
\end{pmatrix} \right| 
$$
and so we get that
$$
\begin{align}
 \Res (f,\, g)  & =-b\left| \begin{pmatrix}
1 & a & b \\
1 & p & q \\
0 & 1 & p
\end{pmatrix} \right|  -q \left| \begin{pmatrix}
1 & a & b \\
0 & 1 & a \\
1 & p & q
\end{pmatrix} \right|  \\
 & =-b\left(  \begin{pmatrix}p & q \\ 1 & p \end{pmatrix} -\begin{pmatrix}a & b \\ 1 & p \end{pmatrix}  \right)-q\left(  \begin{pmatrix}1 & a \\ p & q \end{pmatrix} +\begin{pmatrix}a & b \\ 1 & a \end{pmatrix}  \right) \\
 & =-b(p^{2}-q-ap+b)-q(q-ap+a^{2}-b) \\
 & =-bp^{2}+bq+abp-b^{2}-q^{2}+apq-a^{2}q+bq \\
 b(p^{2}+b)+q(a^{2}+q) & =abp+apq+2bq
 \end{align}
$$
2. 
We have that
$$
\begin{align}
 \Res (aX^{2}+bX+c,\,  X-r)  & =\left| \begin{pmatrix}a & b & c \\ 1 & -r & 0 \\ 0 & 1 & -r \end{pmatrix}  \right|   \\
 & =-\left| \begin{pmatrix}a & c \\ 1 & 0 \end{pmatrix}  \right| -r \left|  \begin{pmatrix}a & b \\ 1 & -r \end{pmatrix}  \right|  \\
 & =-(-c)-r(-ar-b) \\
 & =c+ar^{2}+br
 \end{align}
$$
